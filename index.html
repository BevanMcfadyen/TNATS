Greetings from the Toodyay Naturalists' Club Inc

The Toodyay Naturalists' Club (TNC) is located in an internationally recognised 'global hotspot'.

The club was established in 1968 and its aim is to conserve and promote the unique native flora, fauna and landscape values of Toodyay and the Avon Valley, contributing to their preservation for future generations.

Awards

The Toodyay Naturalists' Club won the John Tonkin Greening Award in 1996.

In 1998 it received an Accredited River Manager Gold Award from the Avon River Management Authority.p

In 2003 it received the Toodyay Shire's Organisation of the Year Award

Two members of the TNC have been recipients of the Western Australian Landcare Awards as 'individual landcarers'. In 2014 Greg Warburton was selected, and 2017 Robert Boase was selected for their contribution to caring for the land.

The Club was nominated for the 2018 WA Regional Achievement and Community Awards, and was selected as a Semi-Finalist.
